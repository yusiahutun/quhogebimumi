Britain's oldest person turns 112 in care home but daughter has to watch through window

February 06, 2024
blog

Britain's oldest person turned 112 in a care home today, but could only celebrate through a window with her family due to coronavirus restrictions.

Sarah Lillian Priest, known as Lillian, marked the milestone at York House Care in Swanage, Dorset.

But unlike previous years where family have been able to shower her with presents and watch her blow out candles on a birthday cake, her daughter was unable to even give her mum a hug or a kiss.

That's because the care home has had to take extra precautions due to coronavirus and lockdown.

However, staff at the home made sure that Lillian still had an enjoyable day by throwing a party for her that was attended by staff and other residents.

Owner Richard Wylie and manager Amanda Mansbridge told the Dorset Echo that they are “very proud” to be looking after Lilian, who has lived in York House for nearly nine years.

Lillian also received a card from the Queen and many more from family and friends as she now becomes the oldest person in the country.

Her daughter, Wendy said: “I think it is an amazing achievement. Mum is an inspiration to us all. She has always been positive and not one to give in and we are all very proud of her.”

York House posted on Facebook: "Today is a very special day indeed for Lillian, celebrating her 112th birthday and is officially the oldest person in the United Kingdom. Lillian has been a part of our family here at York House for 9 years, a huge thank you to all of our truly wonderful team, every one of you, for the kind compassionate care you provide for Lillian. Many Happy Returns Sarah Lillian Priest."

A staff member at York House said: "Her daughter Wendy has been down to see her but she has not been able to come in because of the precautions we have in place due to the coronavirus.

"She can see her mum through the window but unfortunately that is as much as we can do for her today. We have to consider Lilian's health and the wellbeing of the rest of our residents."

Lillian's birthday comes a fortnight after the death of the previous record holder, 112-year-old Joan Hocquard, who passed away at her home in Poole on Saturday, October 24.

The birthday girl was born in Tredegar, South Wales in 1908, and she moved to Bournemouth in 1926. She later moved to Swanage with her late husband Arthur in 1969.

When asked about Lillian's secret to living to such a good age, daughter Wendy revealed that her mum has always been very active and enjoyed walking a lot.

During her life, Lillian and her late husband visited many countries including Russia, Spain, and Australia.

She has two grandchildren, and four great-grandchildren, as well as sister Gwen who is 108.
