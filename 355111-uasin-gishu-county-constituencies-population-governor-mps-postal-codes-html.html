Uasin Gishu County: constituencies, population, governor, MPs, postal codes

September 19, 2024
blog

Among Kenya's 47 counties, Uasin Gishu is one of the most blessed. The county, strategically located on a plateau, along the famous Rift Valley, is home to some of Kenya's top runners. Uasin Gishu County borders Trans Nzoia to the North, Kericho County to the South, Elgeyo-Marakwet and Baringo Counties to the East, Nandi County to the South West and Kakamega County to the West. Politically, the region has been a hot spot for votes, aspiring leaders launching manifestos in a bid to sway the public to believe in their ideologies. Do you want to know more about the county's political leadership and administrative elements?

Uasin Gishu is divided into distinct regions, each boasting of productive lands that have proved to be the breadbasket of Kenya for decades. All these regions today have complex cultural, community and administrative systems, and after realization of the new constitution, things have massively altered.

The topography and geology of Uasin Gishu is an asset to the county's development. They enjoy a well-distributed rainfall pattern, and the terrain is ideal for farming. That is why the Uasin Gishu County population is increasing every year.

Uasin Gishu County information

Uasin Gishu Governor, Jackson Kiplagat Mandago (deputized by Daniel Kiplagat Kiprotich) is a famous personality. His political antics and administrative moves have elevated the status of Uasin Gishu County to one of the most strategic regions in Kenya for various investment activities.

However, who are the Members of Parliament that represent the six constituencies in the region? Do you want to know Uasin Gishu postal codes for the areas they represent?

1. Soy

  • Postal code: 731 – 30100
  • Population: 229, 094
  • Member of Parliament: Caleb Kipkemei Kositany

Soy Constituency in Uasin Gishu County covers an area of approximately 688 square kilometres and is represented in parliament by Caleb Kipkemei of the Jubilee Party. The region was created in 2013 after Eldoret North Constituency was divided into two creating Soy and Turbo.

Turbo Constituency possesses six wards which are Ngenyilel, Tapsagoi, Kiplombe, Huruma, Kapsaos, and Kamagut. The main economic activity in this region is large scale maize and wheat farming. Farmers also rear dairy animals for milk production.

3. Moiben

  • Postal code: 9872 – 30100
  • Population: 181, 338
  • Member of Parliament: Silas Kipkoech Teren

Silas Kipkoech of Jubilee Party represents this 770 square kilometres of land, with rich resources and productive population in parliament. The constituency is North East of Eldoret town and has its headquarters in Moiben Township, a significant part of Uasin Gishu County.

Moiben has five wards which are Sergoit, Karuna Meibeki, Moiben, Tembelio, and Kimumu. All these wards have representation in the Uasin Gishu's County Assembly, helping the MP for the region on various administrative matters.

Sub-counties in Uasin Gishu County

Are you wondering about the number of sub-counties in Uasin Gishu County? The county has a total of six sub-counties, similar to the number of constituencies. These are Turbo, Moiben, Kapseret, Ainabkoi, Kesses and Soy. These were created after the implementation of the new constitution and subsequent creation of the Uasin Gishu County.

Is Eldoret a sub-county?

Eldoret is one of the major towns in Kenya and a principal urban centre in Rift Valley region. The town functions as the headquarters of Uasin Gishu County and is not a sub-county. Eldoret is only part of the administrative units of Uasin Gishu, acting as a significant economic hub for the county.
