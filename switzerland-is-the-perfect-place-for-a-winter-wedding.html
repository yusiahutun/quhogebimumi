6 Reasons Switzerland is the Perfect Place for a Winter Wedding

July 30, 2024
blog

Towering mountain ranges, radiant blue lakes, and charming alpine villages are just a few defining characteristics of Switzerland, a country renowned for its breathtaking natural splendor. It’s a place where time stands still and stress dissipates—and a part of the world where exchanging vows is nothing short of magical.

And though Switzerland is a year-round destination, there’s something about a winter wedding here that feels distinctly otherworldly. If you’re in the initial planning stages, let the glittering snow and unbridled romance of a Swiss winter influence where you choose to say I do. Venue options abound, ranging from a rustic cabin ceremony surrounded by glowing candles and twinkling strung lights, to one of the country’s many luxury hotels for an evening of extravagance and charm.

Ahead, six reasons Switzerland is the perfect place for a winter wedding — plus, an inside look at the properties, planners, and vendors that can help make your special day unforgettable.

The Pristine Mountain Views

Dominik Baur / Courtesy of Switzerland Tourism

A ceremony set-up in the Jungfrau region designed by local vendor GAEA.Design

No matter the time of year, couples can rely on unparalleled views at a wedding in Switzerland. With mountainscapes that nearly take your breath away to picturesque villages and crystal-clear lakes, it’s no wonder the country is a coveted locale for tying the knot. Throughout the winter season, the landscape transforms into a cozy wonderland, complete with glistening snow and an overall feeling of magic.

Top tip: Use the stunning surroundings for every moment of your nuptials, from an outdoor ceremony in the snow to a reception at a cabin with sweeping vistas. Wedding designers like GAEA.Design in Jungfrau and eventfieber in Engadin St. Moritz can help you find the perfect backdrop.

Alpine-Inspired Décor

Dominik Baur / Courtesy of Switzerland Tourism

Dinner in an alpine hut in Engadin St. Moritz designed by local vendor eventfieber

With a wintry, snow-capped backdrop comes the unique opportunity for memorable and immersive wedding decor moments. You can glide down an outdoor aisle lined with sprigs of seasonal flora and clusters of glowing candles tucked into elegant lanterns. Inside, at the reception, treat your guests to a whimsical tablescape courtesy of creative vendors like Passiflora with delicate glassware and gorgeous centerpieces.

Top tip: Bring the outdoors in with white wisps and delicate crystal. Experts like eventfieber and Passiflora are masters of visual beauty.

Old-World Swiss Charm

Dominik Baur / Courtesy of Switzerland Tourism

Decoration horses in Engadin St. Moritz

Though the grandiose mountains and picture-perfect lakes might be the initial details that grab your attention, look further and you’ll discover some rich historical landmarks and locales that are well-suited for a winter wedding. In Egadin St. Moritz, alongside venues like Hotel Restorant Lej da Staz, you can explore a network of romantic churches and chapels from a bygone era, each with a unique story to tell.

Top tip: Up the romance factor with a ride in a horse-drawn sleigh — the storybook town of Engadin St. Moritz, in the heart of the Swiss Alps is, in a word, unforgettable.

Venue Options That Span Centuries

Dominik Baur / Courtesy of Switzerland Tourism

Couple at the dinner hut in Engadin St. Moritz

If you envision a rustic cabin setting for your wedding, Switzerland’s selection of cozy venues will give you plenty of options, like the Boutique Hotel Glacier, a 28-room, idyllic chalet-style hotel that offers distinctive, personalized stays and incredible cuisine. But if all-around opulence and grandeur is what you’re after, you’ll also have your choice of luxury hotels situated in romantic settings — the country’s broad array of wedding locales is one of the many reasons couples choose to celebrate their big day here.

Top tip: Explore your options, each with its own breathtaking view, from high-end hotels to remote huts, to plan a ceremony and a reception that honors the history and modern beauty of the country.

Adrenaline-Inducing Adventures

Dominik Baur / Courtesy of Switzerland Tourism

Couple on a chairlift in Mürren, Jungfrau

Not many destinations can offer exhilarating winter wedding activities like paragliding, bungee jumping, and a helicopter ride—but Switzerland can. Areas such as the Jungfrau Region are like an adult playground and are ideal for thrill-seeking couples. And thanks to event planners in the area like GAEA.Design that can lend in-depth knowledge of your options to help turn your vision into reality, planning can be as elegant and exciting as the event itself.

Top tip: Hire a photographer like Dominik Baur who knows the area and can recommend thrills that will photograph well.

Internationally Renowned Service

Dominik Baur / Courtesy of Switzerland Tourism

Reception décor at Boutique Hotel Glacier in Jungfrau

Globe-trotting couples may already be aware of Switzerland’s reputation as a best-in-class service destination. When you’re high in the mountains, collaborating with locals who know the landscape like the back of their hand is a must. Seasoned event planners such as eventfieber in Engadin St. Moritz and GAEA.Design in the Jungfrau Region have a roster of creative, talented vendors that can customize and perfect your vision for a winter wedding in Switzerland.

Top tip: Work with a planner who knows the ins and outs of the specific regions to harness all the local vendors that know how to make a Switzerland wedding uniquely Swiss (and uniquely incredible).
