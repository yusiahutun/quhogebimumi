Christopher Alan "Tricky" Stewart is an American record producer, record director, musician and publisher. In a career spanning more than 30 years, Stewart has won five Grammy Awards and is responsible for more than 50 million records sold. He is known to have produced many hip hop, R&B and pop singles, usually The-Dream. Some of Stewart's record singles are: "Single Ladies" by Beyoncé, "Umbrella" by Rihanna, "One Time" and "Baby" by Justin Bieber, "Me Against the Music" by Britney Spears, "Just Fine" by Mary J Blige, Mariah Carey "Touch My Body" and "Obsessed", "Ride" by Ciara, "Case of the Ex" by Mýa, "16 @ War" by Karina Pasian and "Your Love" by Nicole Scherzinger. In 2012, he was placed on Billboard's 40 Under 40 list, a list of music executives "driving our industry with their artistic and business vision." In 2020, Stewart signed a publishing contract with Spirit Music Group. The deal covers his future works, as Stewart sold his catalog to Hipgnosis Songs in 2018.
