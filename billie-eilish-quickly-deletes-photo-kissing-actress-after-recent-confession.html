It comes after the songstress recently admitted she was attracted to women “for real” but sad that she finds them “intimidating.”

“I’ve never really felt like I could relate to girls very well. I love them so much. I love them as people,” the celebrity said.

“I’m attracted to them as people. I’m attracted to them for real…,” she said. “I have deep connections with women in my life… I’m physically attracted to them. But I’m also so intimidated by them and their beauty and their presence.”

Despite the confession, Billie hasn’t officially come out as bisexual, gay or queer. The singer has previously declared she was “straight as a ruler” to fans.

In a resurfaced video, Billie says: “I like men, but f***ing girls are sick too so like, I have nothing against girls I just don’t find them …” she trailed off. It seemed she stopped to catch herself and said she is “going to stop talking about that.”

Bad Guy is a ‘stupid’ song

The star, who writes songs with her talented brother Finneas O’Connell, are a power duo. Their lyrics have helped push Billie into the mainstream, where the teen found fame at a young age. However, even if fans love one of her particular songs, Billie is undecided.

Earlier this year, Billie explained how she really felt about her hit 2019 single that catapulted her to the high levels of fame she now has.

“Well, objectively, Bad Guy is like the stupidest song in the world,” Billie admitted during a TV interview with Jimmy Kimmel. However, she doesn’t seem to hate it completely.

She continued: “But it’s really good… it’s just like you have to understand. You have to have, like, humor in it. Like, that song is I’m trolling. That song is supposed to be goofy, but it’s just funny because it’s dumb.”
