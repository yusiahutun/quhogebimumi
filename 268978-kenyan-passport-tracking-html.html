Kenyan passport tracking: How to check status online or via SMS

January 29, 2024
blog

Technology adoption by the Kenyan government has created massive improvements in various institutions and integral service providers. Perhaps the most dominant, effective, and well-received is the passport application and processing upgrade. Although not entirely paperless, it is a digital revolution in the making. The new process has saved Kenyans a lot of time and reduced the infamous queues at Nyayo House. With Kenyan Posta passport tracking, applicants can know whether their passports are ready for pick-up without physically visiting the immigration office.

The updated tracking methods have made it easy for Kenyan residents and diaspora residents to view their applications' progress. Here is a look at the three options on how to track your passport in Kenya.

Posta passport tracking

Kenya's Directorate of Immigration recently partnered with Posta to facilitate passport delivery across the country. This made it possible to track one's document via the Posta website. Here is how to track a passport using a tracking number.

  • Navigate to the booking and collection portal.
  • Enter your 10-digit tracking number
  • Click on "track my passport."
  • The Posta passport tracking portal will present you with a page showing your application's progress.

    • SMS tracking

    SMS passport delivery tracking is one of the best options, considering its convenience. One doesn't require an internet connection to access the service, but only a mobile carrier and a mobile device, regardless of the make or model.

    The tracking service utilizes the short code 2032, and you can do it using the following steps:

  • Open the messages app on your phone and create a new message.
  • On the recipient space, enter 2032 and type the tracking number issued upon submission of your documents.
  • Press the send button once you have verified that all the information has been entered correctly.
  • You will receive a text message showing your document as ready for collection or still in processing.
  • You can utilize the same code to request assistance from the immigration department in Kenya by sending HELP. If you find out that your document isn't ready yet, you can use the same code for further enquiries. Ordinarily, the service costs Ksh.5 only for every SMS sent.

    eCitizen passport tracking

    Today, all passport applications are done online after creating an account on the eCitizen portal. After a successful application, one can track their document's status through the platform when logged in to their accounts. Here are the steps to follow for tracking passport online in Kenya.

  • Log in to eCitizen and navigate to the Directorate of Immigration.
  • On the dashboard, you will see the status of your application (if you have already applied). Common statuses include accepted, rejected, or ready for collection.
  • Since this method has no additional cost, you are free to check your application's progress as frequently as you like.

    • How long does it take to get a Kenyan passport?

    It should take at least ten working days to get your first passport once your form has been physically submitted at the Immigration offices. However, numerous applicants have often reported longer wait times, at times as long as two months.

    Where can Kenyans in the diaspora apply for passports?

    Applicants in the diaspora can have the passport process done at various foreign missions. These include London, Washington DC, Berlin, Pretoria, Dubai, and Paris.

    What is the validity period for a Kenyan passport?

    Kenyan passports have a validity period of ten years. If at any time the document contains no further space for visas, a new one must be obtained.

    How much will I pay for a Kenyan passport?

    According to the Directorate of Immigration Service website, to get a 34-page document, one needed to pay Ksh. 4,550, while for the 50-page one, Ksh. 6,050. The 66-page document cost Ksh. 7,550. However, there have been recent updates regarding the costs.

    According to a special gazette notice dated November 7, 2023, Interior Cabinet Secretary (CS) Prof. Kithure Kindiki declared an increase in the fee for a standard 34-page passport application, raising it from the current Ksh.4,550 to Ksh.7,500.

    How strong is the Kenyan passport?

    The current global ranking of the Kenyan passport is 66th, as per the Passport Index ranking. This ranking is determined by the level of international access it provides to Kenyan passport holders. They enjoy visa-free travel to 78 countries, while requiring a visa for entry into 129 other countries worldwide.

    Are Kenyan passports biometric?

    Modern Kenyan passports have various biometric features in line with the digitisation requirements. The new one contains the holder's bio-data, such as fingerprints, digitally stored photos and signatures.

    Which is the strongest passport in the world?

    Singapore passport holders hold the most travel power, as they can enter 193 nations visa-free or with visa-on-arrival.
