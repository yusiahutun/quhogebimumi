Best hotel bars in London

October 06, 2024
blog
1

Cocktail Lounge, Zetter Townhouse

courtesy Zetter

The eclectically designed Zetter Townhouse has recently undergone renovations, making the interiors all the more eye-catching. Its Cocktail Lounge feels like stepping inside an aristocratic home from a bygone era, with sumptuous velvet sofas, antique tables, taxidermy and open fireplaces. Perfect for rainy days when you want to ignore the outside world, this character-filled enclave is a dependable classic. A new cocktail menu from leading mixologists Matt Whiley and Rich Woods offers added appeal.

Zetter Townhouse, 49-50 St John's Square, Farringdon, EC1V 4JJ

2

The Coral Room, The Bloomsbury Hotel

Courtesy

Maximalism is key at The Coral Room, a highly Instagrammable space on the ground floor of The Bloomsbury Hotel. Its vibrant coral walls, Murano glass chandeliers, colourful art and marble bar are inspired by the glamour of the 1920s, and it certainly makes for a lavish spot to while away an evening. Although it does offer cocktails, English sparkling wines are its speciality, so make a start there and let the bubbles flow.

The Coral Room 16-22 Great Russell Street, Bloomsbury, WC1B 3NN

3

The Rooftop Bar and Terrace, Ace Hotel

Courtesy

London's rooftop bars often become crowded hubs in the summer months, so head to the lesser-known Ace Hotel terrace that sits high up above the Shoreditch rooftops. With live DJs, dancing, Sunday brunch menus and a BBQ offering, this intimate space ticks all the right boxes. For an even better view of the capital's skyline, make sure you look through its giant purple telescope where London's landmarks are clear to see.

Ace Hotel, 100 Shoreditch High St, Hackney, E1 6JQ

4

Lyaness, Sea Containers London

Courtesy

Formerly Dandelyan, which was crowned the World's Best Bar in 2018, Lyaness is a sleek bar with waterfront views that offers the best cocktails in London. Its ethos is to democratise cocktails, so the menu starts with seven base ingredients, of which there are three recommended iterations for each, making for 21 drinks. However, if you want to go off menu, that's fine too - the leading mixologists, which have been pooled from across the country, will happily make you whatever you want using whichever base you like best.

Lyaness, 20 Upper Ground, South Bank, SE1 9PD

5

The Fumoir, Claridge's

Courtesy

Perhaps it's the intimate space of The Fumoir in Claridge's (there's capacity for a maximum of 35), or maybe it's the divine 1930s-inspired decor, but this famously elegant bar remains one of the world's best. This is glamour of the highest degree - photography is not permitted and formal attire is required. Choose from an excellent selection of cognacs, armagnacs, rums, tequilas and ports, or from one of the club's classic cocktails.

Claridge's, Brook Street, Mayfair, London W1K 4HR

6

Double Standard, The Standard

Courtesy

The Standard's London arrival was hugely anticipated and not without reason. Not only are its sleek hotel rooms a staycation draw and its restaurant appealing, but its relaxed bar - Double Standard - offers a lively and laidback evening drinking spot. Think of it as a playful pub serving craft beers, wine and classic cocktails alongside a late-night eating menu starring classics such as fish and chips and mac and cheese. The hotel also plans on opening a rooftop bar in the near future (the date is yet to be confirmed), which will no doubt become a fashion pack favourite.

The Standard, 10 Argyle St, Kings Cross, WC1H 8EG

7

The Library Bar, The Ned

Simon Brown

If you're keen to impress someone, The Ned's refined Library Bar is a perfect destination. The hotel was formerly used as a bank, and this intimate bar boasts many of its original features - marble walls, elegant pillars and double-height windows - along with shelves upon shelves of books. An elegant drinks trolley will be wheeled to your table as you choose from one of the bar's classic cocktails, of which champagne is a key theme, and martinis a speciality. Sit back on one of its luxe velvet chairs and allow the elegance of this intimate bar roll over you.

The Ned, 27 Poultry, London EC2R 8AJ

8

Bloomsbury Club Bar, Bloomsbury London

Simon Brown

The Bloomsbury Club Bar takes its inspiration from the legendary Bloomsbury Set, with cocktails named after famous members from Virginia Woolf to Duncan Grant. Wall-to-wall bookshelves allude to literary greats, while plush leather arm chairs and twinkling fairylights create an inviting, cosy atmosphere. If you want an alcove on the vine-clad terrace, it's advisable to book ahead of time, but the indoor bar with its waistcoated staff and live music is just as alluring.

The Bloomsbury Club Bar, 16-22 Great Russell Street, WC1B 3NN
