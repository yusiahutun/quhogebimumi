Early life

Michelle Grace was born on December 4, 1968, in Chicago, Illinois. Her birth name was Michelle Messer, and she is the daughter of George Messer, who worked as an ad executive, and Marsha Messer.

Michelle grew up in Peoria, Illinois, where she attended Richwoods High School. She was active in sports while in high school and played for her father's fast-pitch team as a batter. She also played for her school's softball team and was an all-city first-base player.

Aside from sports, Michelle was also interested in modelling. She started taking part in beauty pageants at a young age. At just 17 years, she took part in the Miss Illinois competition, finishing among the top 20 finalists. After graduating from high school in 1986, she chose to turn this passion for modelling into a career.

Career

After high school, Michelle quit sports and concentrated on her modelling career. Her performance in the Miss Illinois pageant put her on modelling agents' radar. As a result, she left Peoria with her parent's blessings and moved to Chicago city to further her career.

In Chicago, Michelle started as a model doing commercial ads. She posed for top retail brands such as Sears, Kroger supermarket, and Woolworth. At one time, she also posed for her hometown minor league baseball team, Peoria Chiefs.

While Michelle was a talented model, she always wanted to be an actress. So after some time in Chicago, she moved to Los Angeles in 1990, where she took acting classes at the Los Angeles Actors Group. She also spent some time in the city before shuttling back and forth to Chicago, where her then-husband was living.

Unfortunately for Michelle, it took time before she had any success in the acting world. It wasn't until 1998 that she landed her first role as Judy Campbell in the film, The Rat Pack. Then, in 2000, she got another role in the movie A Rumour of Angels. She also starred in the 2002 TV film, Baseball Wives, where she played Sydney Coopman.

Despite her dreams to be an actress, Michelle had a brief acting portfolio, which ended with her third film role. However, her career in the entertainment industry did not end there; she transitioned into making films.

In 2002, Michelle produced her first film Narc, which featured Hollywood big names such as Ray Liotta, Chi McBride, and Jason Patric. She also served as an executive producer in the film Baseball Wives, where she had an acting role. Other projects she has produced include the films Knights of the South Bronx (2005) and Take the Lead (2006).

Who is Michelle Grace's husband?

The filmmaker is currently not married. However, she has tied the knot twice in the past. Michelle's first marriage was to MLB player Mark Eugene Grace. The couple met in Peoria, Illinois, where Mark was playing for the minor league baseball side, Peoria Chiefs. At the time, Michelle was starting her modelling career and had landed a gig to model for the team.

Though there was suspicion of cheating, the two remained friends until 1997 (four years after Michelle's divorce), when Ray asked her out.

After a brief period of dating, Michelle Grace and Ray Liotta tied the knot in Las Vegas. In 1998, the couple welcomed their first and only child, daughter Karsen Liotta. Michelle's second marriage also ended in divorce. The couple split up in 2004 after seven years of marriage.
