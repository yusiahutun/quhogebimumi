William Ruto Revives Plan to Construct Arror and Kimwarer Dams in New Deal with Italy Gov't

May 05, 2024
blog
  • President William Ruto was implicated in the KSh 63 billion Arror and Kimwarer scandal and was scheduled to testify
  • Ruto came forward and rubbished claims that KSh 21 billion had not been lost in the Arror and Kimware dam project saying the money in question was KSh 7 billion
  • After a meeting with Italian President Sergio Mattarella, Ruto has secured a deal to continue the construction of three dams that had stalled

President William Ruto has struck a deal with visiting Italian leader Sergio Mattarella to restart the construction of three dams in Kenya.

Ruto disclosed that they had signed a pact to fund the construction of the embattled Kimwarer, Arror and Itare dams.

The president said the dams will help the country achieve food security, water delivery to Kenyans and climate action.

"We have committed in good faith to re-establish cooperation on the construction of the Arror, Kimwarer and Itare dams and other water and sanitation projects which are critical in our food security agenda, water delivery and climate action," Ruto said.

Court cases

The head of state also said the court cases surrounding the three dams shall be settled after they agreed to a renegotiation.

"I am proud to announce that the three dams which were subject to court cases and court matters, we have now agreed on a framework to settle all the outstanding court cases and court matters and we have agreed that the facilities that had been provided for the construction of these dams will be renegotiated so that we can provide for the delivery of the dams and the implementation of the projects," the president said.

Speaking at a press briefing in the state house after meeting with his Italian counterpart, Ruto said Mattarella had agreed to advance KSh 14 billion in grants and soft loans to aid the Kenya Kwanza administration deliver its promise to Kenyans.

"The government of Italy has also committed to advance 100 million euros in grants and soft loans in covering projects in agriculture, development of MSMEs, housing and urban settlement, health, digital super highway and the creative economy. This will be done through the Kenya-Italy multi-five-year cooperation strategy on sustainable development," he said.

The president maintained that Italy will remain a valued partner to Kenya in all sectors to ensure the enriching of seamless bilateral ties.

William Ruto: I will construct if elected

In the run-up to the August 9 General Election, in his campaign manifesto, Ruto promised to construct the stalled dams if elected.

Speaking at a camping trail in Elgeyo Marakwet county, where he had camped for his presidential campaigns, Ruto noted the benefit of the projects, which he asserted would benefit locals if implemented.

"With God's grace, come August 9, we will continue with the construction of the two dams. These projects will help the residents get water for their livestock, irrigation and generation of electricity...I would like to challenge anybody who has a problem with Arror and Kimwarer to tell us what the problem is. What happened is political conmanship. If elected, I will continue with the project," said Ruto.
