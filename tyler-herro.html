Tyler Herro is a successful American Professional Basketball player who is currently playing for the Miami Heat of the National Basketball Association(NBA). Tyler Herro played college basketball for the Kentucky Wildcats. He is also nicknamed “Boy Wonder” given by his college friends.

As of now, Tyler is 24 years old. He was born on 20th January 2000 in Milwaukee, Wisconsin. Likewise, he was born to (father) Christopher Herro and (mother) Jennifer Herro. He has grown up with two brothers named Austin Herro and Myles Herro.

Professional Life

Talking about Herro’s professional life, he started playing basketball in his high school (Whitnall) in Greenfield, Wisconsin. Throughout his entire high school career, Herro tallied over 2000 points. Herro averaged 14.0 points, 4.5 rebounds, and 2.5 assists. He did so as the only player to start in all 37 games for the Kentucky Wildcats.

On 14th November 2017, Herro committed to playing college basketball at the University of Kentucky. On 2019, February 27, Herro scored a career-high 29 points in a 70-66 win over Arkansas. Likewise, on April 12, 2019, Herro declared for the 2019 NBA draft, forgoing his final three years of college eligibility and hiring an agent. Furthermore, he was listed as a mid-first-round selection in most mock drafts. Herro was drafted as the 13th pick of the first round by the Miami Heat on June 20, 2019.

Moreover, he signed with the Heat on 10 July 2019, and on 23 October 2019, Herro made his NBA debut. He started with a 120-101 victory over Memphis Grizzlies and he finished that season opener with 14 points, 8 rebounds, 2 steals, and an assist.

On August 12, 2020, he scored a career-high 30 points during a 116–115 loss to the Oklahoma City Thunder.
