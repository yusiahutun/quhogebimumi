What are your favorite dog names? Is the meaning of the name a deciding factor? Well, Swahili names for dogs are many people's favourite because they are not only beautiful but also have a straightforward pronunciation.

There are plenty of Swahili names for dogs you can choose to identify your dog with. However, before you do you need to study your pet, understand their character, then settle on a name.

Swahili names for dogs

Below are some of the most decorated pet names in Kiswahili you can use on pets, preferably dogs. You can use these names to refer to any dog regardless of gender.

  • Hekima - The name refers to being intelligent and knowledgeable. It is suitable for a smart dog that seems brainy
  • Damisi - Damisi refers to a cheerful and bubbly personality. It is a perfect choice for a playful dog
  • Sanura - Meaning intelligent. It befits a wise dog
  • Nuru - Nuru means light. It can be an ideal name for that dog you love the most, one that lights up your life and brings you joy
  • Kali - This is a Swahili word that refers to a fierce animal. It would serve better for your ferocious dog
  • Zaire - As much as the title refers to some African country, it will serve well for your pet as well. Zaire means river

Swahili pet names for female dogs

There are specific names used explicitly on female dogs. Some of them are listed below.

  • Adia - This is a short but meaningful identity you can use for your puppy
  • Tiifu - Though short and funny, the name means faithful and loyal. It is suitable for a dog who's compliant, jolly and affectionate
  • Nalo - Means beach. Perfect for a dog who reminds you of beautiful memories
  • Ashanti - Means "Great African woman". An ideal tag for your female dog
  • Makena - This refers to someone full of delight. A dog who possesses this attribute can earn the name as well
  • Amina - The name means safe and peaceful. Some dogs exhibit these qualities.
  • Asha - Lively. If you have a playful dog, why not name her Asha?
