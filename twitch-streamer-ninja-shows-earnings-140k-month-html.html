Twitch streamer Ninja shows earnings of over $140K per month - and fans lose their minds

January 30, 2024
blog

A popular online streamer accidentally gave fans a sneak peak Wednesday into his Twitch earnings from the last 30 days.

Richard Tyler Blevins, known to the online world as Ninja, was streaming alongside Complexity part-owner Cloazy when he closed out of a tab of the game Fortnite, and showed his desktop with an open Twitch dashboard.

'Ah, you guys got a little sneak peek at the TTV earnings this month, guys,' he said right after he exited out of the dashboard.

The dashboard shows the the number of hours streamed and money earned from October 15-November 15.

Ninja's results show that he had streamed 102 hours within the past month, held 23,828 subscribers, and earned $142,177 in 30 days on the streaming platform which is popular with gamers. 

Richard Tyler Blevins, known to the online world as Ninja, accidently revealed his monthly earnings from Twitch while streaming yesterday

The earnings calculate the amount of money he made, the amount of hours he streamed, and the number of subscribers he's had within the last 30 days

Ninja's fans on X (formerly known as Twitter) were extremely shocked to find out his monthly earnings, and extremely jealous of it as well.

Users tweeted skull and crying emoji's while saying things like, 'bro makes $1400 an hour' and 'He made $60k the day OG Fortnite dropped.'

Other people questioned if Ninja showed his Twitch dashboard on purpose as a way to diss people that may be talking bad about him.

'Had to let the haters know i respect it,' one person tweeted.

Another person wrote: 'Not sure if this is supposed to be a diss, but the man netted over 100k in a month. whoever’s talking down to him wishes they could do that.'

'Accidentally nah bro was just silently flexing on us,' tweeted another user.

Several users on X (formerly known as Twitter) were shocked to learn of his earnings, and were also jealous to see how much money he's made this month from Twitch

Ninja got his fame streaming himself playing video games back in 2017 when Fortnite was hitting its peak in popularity. 

He currently has over 12 million followers on Instagram and over 23 million subscribers on YouTube.
