This vase (14" tall) has the word AMPHORA on the base. I've been told that it's a Russian piece.

E.H., GARDINER, MONT.

Actually, this pottery vase was made by the Amphora Porcelain Works, run by Riessner from 1910 to 1945 in the Teplitz-Turn area of Bohemia, now Trnovany in the Czech Republic. Bohemia was part of Austro-Hungary prior to World War I, and examples from there are marked "Austria." Pieces marked "Czechoslovakia" were made after the war. This circa 1920 vase exemplifies Art Nouveau and Arts and Crafts styles.

Valued at: $1,000
