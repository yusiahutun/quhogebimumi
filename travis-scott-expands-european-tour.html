Travis Scott Expands European Tour

blog

LOS ANGELES (CelebrityAccess) — After fully selling the European leg of his upcoming Utopia – Circus World Maximus Tour, rapper Travis Scott announced a slew of new shows to meet demand on the continent.

The tour, produced by Live Nation, has been expanded to include performances in Lisbon Portugal, Madrid, Spain, Frankfurt, Germany, and Arnhem, Netherlands.

In its current configuration, the European leg of the tour is scheduled to get underway on June 28th at the Gelre Dome in Arnhem and conclude on August 2nd at Lisbon’s Meo Arena.

The new dates are in addition to sold-out shows at venues such as the 43,661-capacity Rhein Energie Stadion in Cologne, Germany, and the 48,300-capacity Deutsche Bank Park in Frankfurt. The tour includes his largest-ever U.K. headline show at the 48,256-capacity Tottenham Hotspur Stadium in London.

For every ticket sold to the tour, Scott and his team will donate 1 Euro/Pound to the Cactus Jack Foundation, a 501(c)3, which helps Houston students through toy drives, scholarship programs to HBCU college students, and fulfilling expenses for education and creative endeavors.

Travis continues to be represented by James Rubin at WME.
