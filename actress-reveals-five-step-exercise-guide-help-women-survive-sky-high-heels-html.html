Gwyneth Paltrow reveals her five-step exercise guide to help women in heels

January 27, 2024
blog

When it comes to life improvement there isn't any challenge that Gwyneth Patrow is unwilling to tackle - and that includes saving women from their killer stilettos.

The 42-year-old actress and lifestyle guru called on professional ballerina-turned-physical therapist Michelle Rodriguez to share her five-step exercise guide with the readers of her lifestyle site Goop, with the aim of providing welcome relief to all those women who struggle to bear the pain that heels can often create.

Ms Rodriguez, who is also the founder of the Manhattan Physio Group, whipped up a series of Gwyneth-approved foot stretches meant to counteract the physical ailments associated with continuously teetering around town in high heels.

GWYNETH PALTROW'S FIVE STRETCHES FOR HIGH HEEL PAIN

INTRINSIC MUSCLES STRETCH  

Ms Rodriguez's first stretch is ideal for combating hammer toes, pinched nerves, stress fractures and bunions

You should start by linking the fingers of your opposite hand in between your toes as if you were shaking hands with your foot. Your thumb should remain on the outside while your other fingers are interlaced with your toes.

The position should be held for 30 seconds before you pull the toes down and hold for another five seconds. The motions are to be repeated 10 times.

FOOT AND ANKLE EXTENSORS STRETCH 

Ms Rodriguez's next move is intended to prevent shin splints and stress fractures.

After kneeling down on both knees, you should put a tightly rolled hand town under your feet where your toes and the top of your foot meet. Once your towel is in position, you need to carefully sit back onto your heels until you feel the stretch across your feet and ankles. You are advised do this move twice and hold the position for 30 seconds each time. 

PERONEALS STRETCH 

Step three tackles your foot and lower leg alignment, as well as dysfunctions in your foot and ankle joints, and requires a strap that will not stretch. A long belt should suffice.

While laying on your back, place the strap around the ball of your foot and lift that leg up. Your other leg should remain extended on the floor. Toes should be stretched towards you before you turn your foot inwards. The strap should be held with the hand opposite the foot that you are stretching. You should hold the move for 30 seconds and repeat it twice for each leg. 

Top tip: Stretches can help counteract the negative effects of ill-fitting shoes

CALF STRETCH 

The following move is just a simple calf stretch meant to strengthen your Achilles heels and protect your calf tears. While standing with your front leg bent and your back leg extended, push your hands against the wall and keep your back knee straight with your heels glued to the ground. After 30 seconds, you should move your extended foot closer to the wall while bending your knee slightly, but keeping both heels on the floor. This position should also be held for 30 seconds. Both moves should then be repeated on your other side. 

FHL STRETCH

Ms Rodriquez's final stretch is supposed to avoid tendinitis and problems with your big toes. This is also an easy move, which only requires you to be close to a door frame or ledge of a wall. Put your big toe against the door or wall and slide it down so it points to the ceiling while you push the ball of your foot towards the ground. You should bend your knee while keeping your other toes and heel flat on the ground.
