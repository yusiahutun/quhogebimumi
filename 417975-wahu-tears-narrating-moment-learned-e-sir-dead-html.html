E-Sir is still being celebrated till today, and on the seventh episode of Nameless and Wahu's reality show This Love, they spoke candidly for the first time on the day the 21-year-old rapper was lost.

On the episode aired on Showmax, Nameless narrated what exactly happened moments before they lost the rapper, who was with him driving back to Nairobi from a concert in Nakuru.

On the fateful day, March 16, 2003, Nameless said they drove for a few kilometres and got to a petrol station. E-Sir asked him to come and sit at the back as he wanted to have a chat.

They drove for a while and got to a lake which E-Sir marvelled at the beauty of the lake while telling the other three, including Nameless, of how nice it looked.

"E-Sir looks through and says, 'Look at that lake it's very beautiful and has a pink reflection, it's beautiful,' then we all looked at the lake and we were like 'wow that's so nice'. When the driver was looking, not concentrating on the road, so he did not know that he was swerving away from the centre. When he could not get control of the car and it started flipping," Nameless narrated.

Wahu learns of accident

The Sweet Love hitmaker said she was at her apartment when she got a call that Nameless had been involved in an accident and that they were all okay.

Wahu got in touch with Nameless's big brother Bob Mathenge, and the two left for Nakuru. When they got to the hospital, they found the guy who was driving crying outside the facility.

Bob said when they got to the ward, Nameless told him that there was a rumour about E-Sir being dead, but he needed to confirm.

"I had to go to the mortuary, first time in there and I had to identify E-Sir.," Bob said.

A sobbing Wahu said Bob came back nodding and confirmed that it was E-Sir in the mortuary, and she could not stop weeping.

Habib learns brother is dead

E-Sir's brother Habib also spoke of how he learnt about his brother's death which was revealed to him by Big Pin, who visited him. When Habib saw the rapper, he knew something was wrong.

"I saw Big Pin coming to our place and he looked different. I had never seen him like that. Big Pin told me E-Sir was dead,' he said.

Habib said he lost a brother but again another in Nameless, who has always been there for him.

Nameless said he wonders what could have been if the rapper was still around.

E-Sir death anniversary

As previously reported by TUKO.co.ke, several artistes visited E-Sir's grave to remember the late on his 18th death anniversary on March 16.

Nonini, Mr Lenny, Talia Oyando, Nameless, Wahu, Big Pin and E-Sir's brother Habib were among artists who paid their tributes.
