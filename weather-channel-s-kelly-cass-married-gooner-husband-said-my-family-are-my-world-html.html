Kelly Cass's career achievements

Weather Channel's icon Kelly Cass has worked as Metereologist for almost two decades. She started her career working as an associate producer and later, weeknight weather weathercaster at WTZA-TV.

But she came into the spotlight as the morning weekday meteorologist at WRGB-TV which she worked for seven years. During this time, she received the New York State Broadcasting Award for "best storm coverage" during the blizzard of 1993.

She, later on, moved to The Weather Channel in 2000 where she has been working since. Her feature report about being selected for a flight in an F-A 18 jet of the Blue Angels unit of the U.S. Navy is considered one of her most enjoyable reports of all time.

A Short Bio of Kelly:

Kelly Cass was born on November 27, 1973. Kelly has received her bachelor’s degree from Adelphi University and a certificate in international studies from Yonsei University, which is located in Seoul, South Korea where she received a red belt In Tae Kwan Do.

Later on, she got a broadcast meteorologist certificate from Mississippi State University eventually finding herself pursuing a career of a Metereologist broadcaster.

Kelly Cass is known as an on-camera meteorologist for the Weather Channel. She serves viewers with weather information every Saturday and Sunday morning on AMHQ Weekends.

Prior working for Weather Channel, she served as a weekday morning meteorologist at WRGB. While working there, she was awarded the New York State Broadcasting Award for Best Storm Coverage during the snowstorm of 1993.
