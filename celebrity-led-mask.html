The A-list are obsessed with these LED face masks

September 06, 2024
blog

If you’ve scrolled through Instagram recently, (who hasn’t) you’ve probably noticed the A-list’s self-care gadget of choice. From Chrissy Teigen to Victoria Beckham and Julia Roberts, Hollywood’s glowiest faces are all treating their skin with an LED face mask. They might look frightening (or, in some cases, perfectly Instagram-worthy), but these futuristic devices are actually the ultimate in at-home skin tech. Just ask makeup artist Quinn Murphy, who prepped Kate Hudson’s skin for the 2021 Met Gala with the CurrentBody Skin LED Light Therapy Mask and CurrentBody Skin The Neck and Dec Perfector. Or fellow Hollywood make-up artist Georgie Eisdell, who treated Carey Mulligan to the same treatment before the 93rd Academy Awards.

"LED masks are a new level of red carpet skin prep," says Murphy, explaining it left Hudson's skin "visibly smoother and more alive" in minutes. Not to mention incandescent. "For an event like the Met Gala, it’s a quick and important step to gorgeous skin. Kate was super excited to try it”.

Eisdell agrees that it's the ultimate pre-event pick-me-up. "Before every red carpet we mask!!" she said on her Instagram on the evening of the Oscars. "The @currentbody LED mask smooths, calms and firms. I cannot live without it. I made sure we got a little 15 minute relaxation and skin love this morning with Carey before we started glam."

So, how do these smart devices work? That depends on the colour of light you choose. Red diodes work by stimulating the skin cells to product collagen, leading to firmer, plumper skin in time, while acne-prone types can call on blue diodes to kill the bacteria that can lead to blemishes. And for the truly serious? Near infra-red, or white light, penetrates deeper than them all, replenishing depleted cells to spark firmer, more resilient skin from the inside out.
