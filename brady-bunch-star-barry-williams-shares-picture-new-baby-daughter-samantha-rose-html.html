He shot to fame as the eldest child of the Brady Bunch.

And now Barry Williams is one of the oldest fathers on the block after having a new daughter at the age of 57.

The jubilant actor has been showing off pictures of ambrosial Samantha Rose as he celebrates becoming a parent for the second time, his first with current girlfriend Elizabeth Kennedy.

Newest member of the bunch: Sitcom star Barry Williams has been showing off his new daughter Samantha Rose

A source told Rumorfix: 'Samantha Rose is Elizabeth and Barry's greatest earthly blessing and they've never been happier.'

It only emerged last month his current squeeze was pregnant, just weeks before she was due to give birth.

However the pair had obviously been preparing, as they had already decided on their daughter's name before she was born.

It is the second child for the former teen idol.

Happy couple: Barry Williams and his much younger girlfriend Elizabeth Kennedy are over the moon

He has a nine-year-old son Brandon with his ex-wife Elia Mary Matt, who he was married to from 1999 until 2005.

Barry also had a previous marriage with Diane Martin from 1990 until 1992, but they did not have any children.

He claimed he had dated his on-screen mother  Florence Henderson in his book Growing up Brady.

However she later said their romance was 'an innocent dinner and ended with a kiss on the cheek.'

She also said in her own memoir: 'Barry did have a serious crush on me, which I understood and helped him get past.

'Let us just say that if he had entertained a roll in the hay with me, I would never have done it.'

Barry decided as a child he wanted to be an actor, and made his television debut in an episode of Dragnet in 1967.

After appearing in a slew of other shows, he got his big break when he was cast as eldest son Greg on iconic sitcom The Brady Bunch in 1969.

The comedy followed the antics of the titular family, which becomes six siblings strong after widowed architect Mike Brady marries Carol Ann Martin.

The producers intended her to be a divorcee, but left her history ambiguous after protests from the show's network ABC.

Greg was a key member of the cast, and as he grew up was portrayed as the show's teen idol, with his character becoming a ladies man at school, and attempting to launch a career as a singer.

It ran for five seasons, before finally finishing up in 1974, but returned in 1981 reunion movie The Brady Girls Get Married and 1988 special A Very Brady Christmas.

In recent years Barry made a guest appearance on That '70s Show, Celebrity Rehab With Dr Drew, and starred in Camp Playboy.

A particularly bizarre career twist saw him lose a celebrity boxing match to former Partridge Family star Danny Bonaduce.
