Wimbledon 2016 star Agnieszka Radwanska's naked photo shoot revealed

February 18, 2024
blog

Naked pictures of Wimbledon number three seed Agnieska Radwanska stoked a long-running feud with her tennis coach father, the star's mother revealed today.

Agnieszka, now 27, took part in the risqué photoshoot in an apparent act of rebellion because Robert Radwanski had left her mother for another woman.

Now Marta Radwanska has claimed her daughter had had enough of Robert's strict, demanding regime, that he refused to let his daughters have a boyfriend - and that she found the controversial naked images 'in good taste'.

Marta, 49, told MailOnline: '[Robert] was very strict and demanding. He shouted at her on the courts and would always get angry.'

Special bond: Agnieszka and her tennis-playing sister Ula, left, made up for lost time when Agnieska dumped her father as a coach and their social media pages reveal they are very close

Demanding: Robert Radwanksi, pictured in khaki shorts walking behind Agnieszka in July 2012, shortly before they fell out over his domineering style and strict methods

'He didn't want her to have a boyfriend and wanted her to get married as late as possible so it wouldn't interfere with her tennis career.

'Eventually, she had enough.

'There were many controversies about her naked pics, but honestly they didn't uncover much.

'She thought about it for a long time and when she showed me the photos of other athletes who had been in the magazine I realised that it was prestigious and in good taste so told her to go ahead with it.

'Her father wasn't happy.'

Radwanska, who plays Ukrainian Kateryna Kozlova at Wimbledon today, dumped her tennis coach father in late 2011 and went on to enjoy success with Tomasz Wiktorowski, Poland's Fed Cup captain.

She made a veiled reference to the problems she had with her father in an interview in 2012, paying tribute to his commitment but revealing they had personal issues she could not overcome. 

'On the court we always had a good relationship and he's a great coach,' Radwanska said. 'But sometimes it gets too much when you mix up your private life with tennis. Professional tennis is always a lot of stress and emotions.'

Nicknamed 'The Professor' by her tennis pals because of the intelligence of her game, Agnieszka began hitting sponge balls almost before she could walk, together with her younger sister Ula. 

The pair were born in Krakow, Poland, into a strict, Catholic family. 

Mr Radwanski began teaching Agnieszka and her younger sister Urszula when they were just babies.

Agnieszka's mum said: 'He bought her a small tennis racket and some sponge balls when she was 1.5 years old.

'We were living in Germany at the time and my ex husband was a tennis coach.

'She started so early that she didn't have time to do anything else.

'At first she was just bouncing the balls on the racket. Later she practised by hitting them against a wall.

'She started to play when she was three-years-old and when she was six she won her first tournament.

'Urszula was 4.5-years-old then and she finished in 3rd or 4th place.

Tender: Since dumping her father as her coach, Agnieszka found love with partner Dawid Celt, who used to be a tennis sparring partner

'There was of course rivalry between them, but it was ok as it gave them more motivation.

'Ula wanted very much to be the same as Aga.

'Unfortunately, Ula has had bad luck. She's already had 3 operations for torn ligaments.

'Then her leg was in plaster, she was on crutches and couldn't play for three months.

'Now she wants to play in smaller tournaments to get back into shape and higher in the rankings.'

As Agnieszka went from strength to strength she eventually turned pro in 2005 at the age of 16 following an impressive performance during the Wimbledon junior singles.

Just two years later she became the first Polish tennis player in history to receive the WTA tour singles title and in 2012 she became world No.2 after beating Julia Gorges during the Dubai Tennis Championships and Maria Kirilenko in Wimbledon.

She is now estimated to be worth £10.8 million.

But in the background of her success lurked the dark shadow of her hyper-critical dad.

Close: The pair have stayed very close and share tips on everything from tennis to makeup to fashion

Things eventually came to a head in 2010 following an airplane crash that killed Poland's then prime minister and 95 high-ranking government officials on their way to the 70th anniversary of a WWII massacre near the city of Smolensk in Russia.

A staunch right-winger, he refused to accept that the crash was an accident and began posting political comments on her website and suggesting that they were from her.

Her mother said: 'She was very angry when her father began putting up these political views on her website.

'She is not interested in politics, you can not combine sports with politics, and they were not her views.

'She doesn't have time to be interested in politics.'

The following year, Mr Radwanska left Agnieszka's mum for another woman and Agnieszka banned him from going to tournaments with her.

Two years later she dumped him as her coach.

Agnieszka's mum explained: 'She had had enough. You cannot train under such stress.'

She added: 'I was definitely softer on the girls, Robert was more demanding, but it was because he was also their coach.

'I know many players who were coached by their dad and sooner or later there is always a conflict. Its not so easy.

'When kids are small its ok, but later relationships become difficult.

'A father is demanding more than a normal coach, because he wants everything to be the best.

'A coach who works for money never puts as much heart into it.'

Talented: Agnieszka's sister Ula is a gifted player but has struggled with injury, although she now wants to up her game and participation in tournaments

In the same year she got together with her long-term boyfriend Dawid Celt, who used to be a tennis sparring partner and began having fun with blonde sister Urszula, 25, also a professional tennis player.

It was also when she appeared naked floating on a swimming pool littered with tennis balls for ESPN magazine, a move that saw her being dropped as ambassador for a catholic youth group in Poland and further the conflict between her and her dad.

Unapologetic, Agnieszka retorted: 'If I was invited to do it again, I would do it again.'

But despite her father's reported fury over the photoshoot, her mother defended the star, saying: 'There were many controversies about her naked pics, but honestly they didn't uncover much.

'She thought about it for a long time and when she showed me the photos of other athletes who had been in the magazine I realised that it was prestigious and in good taste so told her to go ahead with it.

'I guess her father wasn't happy though.'

And now, three years into her relationship with Dawid Celt, her mum hinted the Wimbledon hopeful may be looking to tie the knot.

Fun: After Agnieszka dumped her strict father as a coach, the girls made up for lost time and posted pictures of themselves going out and having fun

Marta said: 'He's a nice boy. She wants to have a family some day and after a quarter of a century of playing tennis she will certainly not be playing as long as Serena to when she's 35.

'Probably around 30 she will want to finish but it all depends on how she feels.'

And now it looks like Agnieszka and her dad may be slowly reconciling their differences following the death of her grandfather, a former ice-hokey player and PE teacher who had helped finance the sisters' early tennis careers by selling off a collection of valuable paintings.

At the beginning of this month (June) Agnieszka attended a memorial for her late granddad, to whom she was very close and who died of cancer, along with sister Ula and their dad Robert.

Marta said: 'After the Paris tournament Agnieszka was in Krakow for a few days and they met.

'She visited a tournament organised by him in memory of her grandfather.

'She took some pictures with fans and after that she visited me.

'They are getting better.' 

Robert Radwanksi declined to comment to MailOnline.
