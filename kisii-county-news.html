Kisii County is located in the southwestern part of Kenya and is known for its lush green landscapes, vibrant culture, and friendly people.

The county shares borders with Nyamira County to the northeast, Narok County to the south, and Homa Bay and Migori Counties to the west.

History

The Kisii people are western Bantu speakers who are known for soapstone carvings and farming of bananas and indigenous vegetables. The county was originally established by British soldiers who were being forced to retreat from Lake Victoria by heavy gunfire from German soldiers’ gunboats during the Great War in the early 20th century.

Kisii County Public Service Board

The Kisii County Public Service Board is responsible for human resource management and promotion of values & principles of public service at the County Government of Kisii.

Its core functions include establishing and abolishing offices in the County public service, appointing persons to hold or act in offices of the County public service, exercising disciplinary control, promoting values and principles in the County public service, developing coherent, integrated human resource planning and budgeting for personnel emoluments, preparing regular reports to the County Assembly, issuing advisories to the County Government on human resource management, making recommendations to the Salaries and Remuneration Commission on remuneration, pension and gratuities of public officers.

County Government of Kisii

The county government of Kisii is headed by Governor Simba Arati who was elected on August 12th, 2022 on an ODM ticket.

The county has nine constituencies, eleven sub-counties, forty-five county assembly wards, one hundred and three locations and two hundred and thirty-seven sub-locations.

The major economy is subsistence farming with bananas being a staple food. Other crops include tea, coffee, tomatoes, vegetables, dairy products, maize, and sugarcane.
