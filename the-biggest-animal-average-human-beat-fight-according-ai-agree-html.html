'What's the biggest animal you could beat in a fight?' is a question regularly posed on social media. 

Earlier this year, one user tweeted the question, and received very different responses. 

One user wasn't too confident, suggesting a crab, while another claimed they could take on a goat. 

Meanwhile, others were feeling braver, with one joking: 'Giraffes got nothing on me', and another writing: 'A gorilla, no kidding. I've been practicing MMA for 6 years now, boxing in parallel for 7 years, I could.'

To settle the debate, MailOnline asked ChatGPT the question, and within seconds, the AI bot began typing out its response. 

Firstly, the bot highlights that there would be several variables to take into consideration.      

'The outcome of a fight between a human and an animal largely depends on various factors such as the size, strength, and abilities of the animal, as well as the specific circumstances of the fight,' it said. 

In general, it claims that humans are not well equipped to physically fight large, powerful animals. 

'Even smaller animals can pose a significant threat due to their speed, agility, and natural weaponry,' it added.
