Kate Middleton Looks Incredible in an Orange Knit Sweater and Skirt

June 15, 2024
blog

The Princess of Wales is having a stylish start to the New Year.

Princess Kate ventured on a solo outing today to Foxcubs Nursery in London, an early years service run by the Early Years Alliance. The nursery, which looks after children between the ages of two and five, offers placements for families eligible for free childcare.

During her visit, Kate—who has long been a champion of early years advocacy—spoke with educators, parents, and children about their experiences.

For the outing, Kate wore a vibrant orange ribbed knit set, composed of a silk-and-cashmere turtleneck sweater and matching high-rise midi skirt. The look, designed by Gabriela Hearst, was complemented by a thick black belt cinched at her waist and black suede boots. The royal stayed warm outside by layering up in a long camel coat.

Today's outing marks another step in the Princess of Wales's charitable work on promoting the importance of early childhood, her signature cause for more than a decade.

Back in 2021, Kate announced the launch of The Royal Foundation Centre for Early Childhood, a hub within the Waleses' charitable vehicle that pushes and facilitates new research on the impact of the early years.

JUSTIN TALLIS//Getty Images

WPA Pool//Getty Images

Last November, Kate wrote an op-ed for The Telegraph, in which she wrote of the urgency to create a "healthier and happier society for future generations."

"If we are going to tackle the sorts of complex challenges we face today like homelessness, violence and addiction, which are so often underpinned by poverty and poor mental health, we have to fully appreciate those most preventative years and do everything we can to nurture our children and those who care for them," she wrote.

"That is why I am determined to continue to shine a light on this issue and to do everything I can to secure much greater focus on those first crucial few years for the youngest members of our society—they are, after all, our future," she concluded.
