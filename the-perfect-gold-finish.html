How To Gild a Frame

July 27, 2024
blog

Rachel Paxton, the author behind Maison de Pax, used to live in Paris. And she's had an affinity for the refined gilded furnishings that grace nearly every Parisian apartment since her time abroad, like the perfectly aged, extremely ornate, beautifully gilded mirror Rachel remembers sitting atop the mantel in the home she and her family rented. But what's a girl to do when she doesn't have thousands to shell out for a gilded mirror? She tries one of Rachel's techniques to add a touch of antiqued metallic glamour to her home.

Try one of these Rachel-tested-and-approved techniques to go for the gold:

1. Paint it. To gild two frames for her guest room, Rachel started with inexpensive frames, painted them using a pale blue-green chalk paint (to fake a patina), then applied a light coat of plain gold craft paint on top. To soften the shine, Rachel added a dark stain, wiping it on, then rubbing it off. Finally, a very light brushing of Miss Mustard Seed's Antiquing Wax gave the piece an aged look.

2.
Wax it. Rachel used Country Chic's Gold Wax to give a frame on her stairway a glittery metallic gold makeover. She says it only took her a few minutes to apply the wax with a lint-free cloth. Try this on an entire piece or add just a few highlights to a furnishing to make it look as though the gold is wearing away.

3.
Rub n' Buff it. Rachel only used a dime-size amount of Rub n' Buff's gold leaf wax on a mirror she bought at Marshalls five years ago for less than $40. She applied it on a rag and wiped off the excess. It was that easy.
