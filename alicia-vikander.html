Professional Career

Alicia Vikander began her professional career at the age of seven in a production of “Kristina Fran Duvemala” at The Goteborg Opera, following which she appeared in other musicals at the Opera, including “The Sound of Music” and “Les Miserables.”

Then, from 2008 to 2010, she starred in the Swedish TV drama “Andra Avenyn” and gained critical praise for her feature film debut as Katarina in the Swedish film “Pure” (2010). In 2011, she played Fragancia Fernandez, a woman arrested for attempted murder in “The Crown Jewels.”

Later, in 2012, she received worldwide notice for her performance as Kitty in the English-language film adaptation of “Anna Karenina.” In the same year, she added Danish to her repertoire by co-starring as Queen Caroline Mathilde in the Nikolaj Arcel feature film “A Royal Affair.”
