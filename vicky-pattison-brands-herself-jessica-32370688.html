Vicky Pattison brands herself 'Jessica Rabbit' as she wows in plunging dress

April 08, 2024
blog

Vicky Pattison wowed her followers after sharing an incredibly glamorous snap online.

The 36-year-old took to Instagram to post her look from Friday night's Comic Relief, and she kept in keeping with the colours. Matching Red Nose Day, Vicky opted for a red gown and caught all the eyes in the process.

The skintight gown showed Vicky's every curve as she ditched her bra in the plunging dress. She held her hands by her waist as she looked directly into the camera and snapped the photo.

With her brunette hair dropping past her chest in a bouncy wave, Vicky certainly knew how to capture the attention of her followers. To complete the look, she opted for a smokey hue on her eyes and a subtle nude lip gloss on her pout.

A light pink was swiped across her nails as each part of the look came together to complement the entire ensemble.

As she shared the snap online, Vicky wrote: "Channelling my inner Jessica Rabbit for Comic Relief tonight."

Her incredibly glamorous appearance came with a flood of love from her followers as one user wrote: "Oh WOW Vicky you look INCREDIBLE." While another said: "You looked absolutely stunning."

A third person wrote: "You look amazing, red really suits you," while a fourth said: "Absolutely unreal."

Vicky joined Laura Whitmore, Alex Scott and Sarah Davies in a big Comic Relief challenge. On the BBC YouTube channel they describe it as: "Laura Whitmore, Alex Scott, Sara Davies and Vicky Pattison take part in Comic Relief’s coldest ever challenge, attempting a 50km sub-zero Arctic expedition in just four days. #SnowGoingBack."

Various celebs took part in different challenges for Comic Relief and through a mixture of all the hard work and effort that went into it, a big lump sum was raised.

In total a whopping £38million was raised which was a huge success from all involved, which included Vicky and co, the Gladiators, Mollie King and Sir Lenny Henry was at the help for the last time before he steps down.
