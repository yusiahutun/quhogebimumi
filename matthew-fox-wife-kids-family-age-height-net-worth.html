Matthew Fox Bio (Age)

Matthew Fox was born on July 14, 1966, and went to Deerfield Academy for a year as a postgraduate, graduating in 1984. For college, he attended Columbia University and graduated with a B.A. in Economics in 1989.

Nothing in Matthew Fox’s upbringing indicated that he wanted to be an actor, but he ended up there anyway. At the age of 25, Matthew Fox made his acting debut in an episode of Wings. In the same year, he played the lead role in a short-lived drama series, Freshman Dorm. Although he was given roles, he was not a familiar face on the small screen and was therefore repeatedly cast in supporting roles before he made his first appearance on the big screen in My Boyfriend’s Back in 1993.

In 1994, Matthew Fox was cast in one of the most prominent roles of his career when Charlie Salinger, the oldest of five siblings who lose both parents in a car accident in the 1990s teen drama Party of Five, was killed. It was a show that produced other stars including Scott Wolf, Jennifer Love Hewitt, Lacey Chabert, and Neve Campbell. Fox, who declared after “Lost” that he was done with television shows, has since appeared in several films. In 2006, he starred in the sports drama We Are Marshall with Matthew McConaughey and had a brief appearance in the 2013 film World War Z starring Brad Pitt.

While his acting career may have stalled, Matthew finds his other passion for photography. A bonus disc released along with The Complete First Series of Lost featured “The Art of Matthew Fox,” which featured pictures he took of the cast and crew while on set.
